Integrating Model-based Prior with Reinforcement Learning for Bipedal Locomotion on Challenging Terrains

Shilong Sun1,2, Chiyao Li1, Haodong Huang1, Zida Zhao1, Dachuan Liu1, Qingbin Gao1, Yuanpeng Wang1, Wenfu Xu1,2
1Harbin Institute of Technology(Shenzhen), 2Guangdong Provincial Key Laboratory of Intelligent Morphing Mechanisms and Adaptive Robotics
Paper Video Code

PTRL Reinforcement Learning and Transfer Learning.

Abstract

With the rapid development of data-driven learning methods, reinforcement learning (RL) has been widely regarded as a potentially effective solution for motion control in legged robots.

However, training usable policies for a single robot typically requires substantial computational resources, and the resulting models are often highly specialized, making them difficult to directly apply to other robots. To address this issue, this paper proposes a novel Prior Transfer Reinforcement Learning (PTSL) framework to tackle the challenge of cross-robot policy transfer. Drawing inspiration from model transfer in deep learning and fine-tuning in natural language processing, we first train a policy for one robot using the Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) algorithm. The actor model of this robot is then transferred to another robot, where certain network layers are frozen to reduce the number of trainable parameters and the training time, while effectively utilizing prior knowledge.

We conducted transfer experiments on various robotic platforms to validate the effectiveness of the proposed approach and systematically investigated the impact of freezing different numbers of layers on the experimental results. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method significantly improves the performance of legged robots in walking tasks, showcasing strong applicability and advantages.

Training Pipeline

SLRT (Source Legged Robot Training): First, train the Go2 model, and then extract the actor network from it.

TLRT (Target Legged Robot Training): The main goal of TLRT is to fine-tune the model by incorporating the actor network parameters from SLRT, thereby leveraging prior knowledge.
